## Content & Copy Rules

These rules apply to ALL content produced for Paepoi and its clients:

1. **No AI-sounding language.** Copy should read like a sharp human wrote it. Avoid filler phrases like "in today's digital landscape," "leverage," "harness the power of," "dive deep," "at the end of the day."
2. **No em dashes.** Use commas, periods, or colons instead.
3. **Short, punchy lines.** Lead with a bold statement. Follow with the strategic "so what" (why the reader should care).
4. **Image copy must standalone.** Any text on a visual should work as a complete thought without needing the surrounding context.
5. **Visual text should be brief.** If it can't be read at a glance, it's too long.
6. **No generic CTAs.** "Learn More" is fine for navigation but not for campaign content. CTAs should reflect the specific value proposition.

## Working Preferences

- **Options before execution.** Present 2-3 directions before building anything out.
- **Approve concepts first.** Don't go deep on production until the concept is locked.
- **Iterate through targeted edits.** Don't rewrite from scratch unless explicitly asked. Make surgical changes.
- **Show your thinking.** When proposing content or strategy, include the rationale (not just the output).
- **Be direct.** Skip the preamble. Lead with the answer or recommendation.

## SEO Content Approach

- Content should target specific, relevant keywords without keyword stuffing.
- Every blog post or article should have a clear intent: inform, convert, or build authority.
- Prioritize original thinking and first-hand expertise over generic roundups.
- Structure content for readability: clear headers, short paragraphs, scannable format.
- Internal linking strategy should connect related content pieces.

## Social Media Approach

- Platform-first thinking. What works on LinkedIn doesn't work on Instagram.
- LinkedIn content should lead with insight or a strong opinion, not a company update.
- Visuals should be clean, branded, and designed to stop the scroll.
- Engagement > reach. Quality conversations over vanity metrics.
